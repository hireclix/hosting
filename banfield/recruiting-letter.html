Dear Dr. [recipient-first-name],
 
I know at one time you were a part of our practice. Since your departure, many new and exciting things have been happening that I would like to share with you.

At Banfield Pet Hospital, we’ve made great strides in building a culture of health and well-being. As part of this pledge, we’re developing a multifaceted, robust health and well-being strategy slated to roll out over the next three years. This new program will build upon work already underway and expand efforts across the practice and includes a holistic definition of well-being comprised of five essentials: mind, body, financial, career, and community.

With Banfield, you can now expect more than what you may have experienced. Read on to see what’s new!
 
Banfield launches Veterinary Student Debt Relief Pilot Program
This past November, we introduced the Veterinary Student Debt Relief Pilot Program, available to full-time Banfield veterinarians. The pilot offers eligible veterinarians: a low interest refinancing option with supplementary interest rate reduction of .25% from a third-party financial institution, a monthly student loan contribution of $150 paid by Banfield directly to qualifying student loans.
 
Banfield announces additional holiday closure
We will be closing all hospitals on Christmans Eve, Christmas Day and New Year’s Day, providing an additional day away from work for all of our associates. We know well-being matters and we are constantly evaluating areas where, as a practice, we can improve flexibility and associate engagement.
 
Banfield brings well-being continuing education to the profession
In 2018, we’ll launch virtual trainings on stress resilience and confronting conflict with teams practice-wide. Our goal will be to help veterinary teams better understand how job-related stress impacts their physical and mental well-being, as well as provide coping mechanisms and tools to build resilience. 
 
Banfield changes how associates are paid during emergencies
Grounded in our Associate Concept and Mutuality Principle, we will pay associates for up to five days if their hospital is closed due to a natural disaster.
 
Banfield tests operating hours in a mutual way
Banfield is starting a new test to evaluate if we can mutually meet our associate, client, and pet needs through shorter operating hours. This large test will involve hospitals in 12 markets closing at 6 p.m. on weekdays, 5 p.m. on Saturday, and entirely on Sunday.
 
I am proud of how far our practice has come in our doctor well being journey and am excited about our future. If you have any interest in exploring opportunities to rejoin our practice, please reach out to me. Your talents would help us make a better world for pets.
 
Best regards,
 
 
George Melillo, VMD
Vice President Veterinary Quality-Northeast
