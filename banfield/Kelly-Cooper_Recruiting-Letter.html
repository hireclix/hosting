Dear Dr. [recipient-first-name],
 
I know at one time you were a part of our practice, and since your departure, we have been asking our doctors for ways to make the practice better for the people and pets who bring our work to life.

I wanted to share with you that we listened to doctors like you about the areas that challenged you most, both inside and outside of the hospital. Challenges like wellbeing, compassion fatigue, flexible schedules, student loan burden, and equipment upgrades were top of mind for you. So we decided to make them a priority focus here at Banfield.
 
Banfield launches Veterinary Student Debt Relief Pilot Program
To address financial burden, this past November we introduced the Veterinary Student Debt Relief Pilot Program, available to full-time (30+ hrs/week) Banfield veterinarians.
 
We’re able to be truly flexible with scheduled hours.
We offer part-time positions, full-time positions, benefits starting at 3 days/week and extra day pay options.
 
Our quality journey is a true highlight as well.
We updated our anesthesia protocols and added a groundbreaking patient safety event reporting system to ensure optimal safety. We also added new CBC machines and anesthesia monitoring systems with capnography in all of our hospitals.
 
Our safety and quality journey will continue to evolve.
This year, we are adding dental radiography to our hospitals. We are continuously reviewing and updating our equipment and medical guidelines.
 
Banfield announces additional holiday closure
Banfield is now closing all hospitals on Christmas Eve, Christmas Day and New Year’s Day, providing additional days away from work for all of our associates. We will also pay associates for up to five days if their hospital is closed due to a natural disaster.
 
Banfield brings well-being continuing education to the profession
This year, we are launching virtual trainings on stress resilience and confronting conflict with teams practice-wide. Our goal is to help veterinary teams better understand how job-related stress impacts their physical and mental wellbeing, as well as provide coping mechanisms and tools to build resilience.
 
 
We have in-depth resources and communities available, created and led by our own doctors, that are helpful in supporting compassion fatigue and quality of life concerns.

We are dedicated as a practice to ask for and listen to the concerns of our doctors. Even though your career has taken you elsewhere for now, I’d love to hear from you about how you think we can continue to bring our mission to life.

Please let me know a good time to connect with you, thank you for your consideration!
 
Sincerely,
 
 
Kelly Cooper, DVM
Vice President Veterinary Quality, Northwest Region
