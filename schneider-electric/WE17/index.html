Schneider Electric Partners with SWE

As a member of the Society of Women Engineers Corporate Partnership Council since 2016, Schneider Electric commits to creating opportunities and supporting diversity for women engineers and technologists.

Connect with us at WE17 by listening to one of our speaker sessions, exploring engineering for kids at Invent it. Build it. or visit us at booth 1535 to discuss your next career move—you can even sign up for one of on-site interview slots! We’re looking for innovative individuals to join us to help us ensure Life is On across the world.

Additionally, through our Return2Work program, Schneider Electric is one of six companies partnering with SWE and iRelaunch within the STEM Re-entry Task Force initiative creating opportunities for engineers who are interested in getting back to their technical careers.

Be a part of the future and drive the innovation in energy management and automation. Discover how Schneider Electric leads a new world of energy with connected products, edge control, and apps, analytics and services.

Invent it. Build it.

Stop by booth 4102 for a fun activity for all ages. We aim to encourage girls to choose a career in engineering and support parents and educators through the learning process.

All girls registered for the SWE Girls Program will participate in a hands-on engineering experience with members of the Society of Women Engineers. While the girls are having fun building and inventing, parents and educators will learn about engineering careers, scholarships, college admission and resources.

Both groups will learn about engineering clubs, camps, afterschool programs and competitions from a wide variety of exhibitors at the Invent it. Build it. EXPO. After the SWE Girls and Parent Educator Program have walked through the EXPO we will open the doors to everyone else.

Superior Work Life Balance

At Schneider Electric, our technologies ensure that Life Is On for everyone, everywhere, at every moment. Life requires you to be present for your loved ones. We want our employees to be present for their life’s significant moments. That’s why we’ve rethought our approach to family leave. Introducing our new commitment—a family leave policy that’s inclusive, gender neutral and addresses needs at different stages in your life.


Schneider Electric North America now offers:

  • 12 weeks of fully paid leave for primary care-giver (birth or adoption)

  • 2 weeks of fully paid leave for secondary care-giver (birth or adoption)

  • 1 week of care leave for elderly or critically ill family members (starting January 1, 2018)

  • 1 week of bereavement for immediate family

  • 2 floating days that can be used for cultural activities

Return2Work

Reconnecting to your career ambitions

Schneider Electric is looking for experienced professionals who took an employment break of three or more years in their field and are looking to re-launch their careers. Return2Work is a professional paid program through which you’ll polish your skills, acquire new ones, and restore the confidence you need to launch a new phase in your professional journey. After the program, the program management will work individually with each candidate to explore full time employment opportunities with Schneider Electric, the global specialist in energy management and automation.

Take advantage of comprehensive training, technical and professional development opportunities and networking. Return2Work will prepare you for a full-time career in critical roles at Schneider Electric.

Dr. Laurie Addison-Lavelle


Dr. Laurie Addison-Lavelle is a senior director of marketing for a large, multi-national technology business, Schneider Electric Software, with responsibility for global and regional marketing strategy and execution. This includes overseeing staff members in Asia-Pacific, India, Europe, North America, Latin America, and the Middle East. Laurie earned her doctorate in Organizational Leadership from Pepperdine University’s Graduate School of Education and Psychology, with focus subjects of millennial women in leadership, cross-generational workforce, and senior leadership.

Her dissertation studies focused on Millennial Women in Leadership-A New General of Women Still Facing Gender Inequality in Business Leadership. She is a member of, and supports many organizations focused on uplifting girls and women including: Women in Schneider Electric, Women in Technology, United Nations’ HeforShe gender solidarity initiative, Room to Read, Women for Women International, Half the Sky, and Water.org.

Laurie currently resides in Southern California, and is a life-long learner with deep passion for educating and motivating men and women on topics to help better understand and create awareness, and lead themselves and their teams toward a healthier, more engaged, and equal work environment.
